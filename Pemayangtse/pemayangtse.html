About Pemayangtse Monastery

Pemayangtse Monastery, located near Pelling in West Sikkim, is one of the oldest and most prestigious monasteries of the Nyingma order of Tibetan Buddhism. It was founded in the 17th century by Lhatsun Chenpo, one of the revered lamas who helped consecrate Sikkim’s first Chogyal (king).

The monastery is renowned for its three-story structure adorned with exquisite wall paintings, sculptures, and an elaborate wooden model of the celestial palace, ‘Zangdok Palri,’ crafted entirely by hand. This unique masterpiece took years to complete and is regarded as a sacred treasure of the monastery.

Overlooking the snow-clad peaks of Kanchenjunga, Pemayangtse is not just a religious center but also a place of unmatched scenic beauty and cultural significance.
