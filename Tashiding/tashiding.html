About Tashiding Monastery

Tashiding Monastery, perched atop a heart-shaped hill, is one of the most sacred and venerated monasteries in Sikkim. Founded in the 17th century by Ngadak Sempa Chempo Phunshok Rigzing, it is built on a site believed to have been blessed by the great Guru Padmasambhava. As part of the Nyingma order of Tibetan Buddhism, it is considered the spiritual heart of Sikkim and a major center for pilgrimage.

The monastery is renowned for its collection of sacred chortens, most notably the Thong-Wa-Rang-Dol, which is believed to cleanse the sins of anyone who gazes upon it. It also holds the Bhumchu, a sacred pot of water used for divine prophecy during the annual festival. Tashiding stands as a vital center of pilgrimage, upholding ancient Nyingma traditions and sacred rituals.

Visitors are captivated not only by its profound spiritual aura but also by the spectacular panoramic views of the confluence of the Rathong chu and Rangeet rivers, with the majestic peak of Mount Kanchenjunga often gracing the horizon.
