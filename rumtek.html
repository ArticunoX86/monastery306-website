Rumtek Monastery

About Rumtek Monastery

Rumtek Monastery, also known as the Dharma Chakra Centre, is one of the largest and most significant monasteries in Sikkim. Built in the 16th century by the 9th Karmapa and later rebuilt by the 16th Karmapa in the 1960s, it is the seat of the Kagyu lineage of Tibetan Buddhism outside Tibet.

The monastery houses many sacred relics, intricate thangkas, and unique statues. Its golden stupa contains the relics of the 16th Karmapa. Rumtek also serves as a vibrant hub of Buddhist learning, meditation, and cultural preservation.

Visitors are captivated not only by its religious significance but also by the breathtaking panoramic views of Gangtok and the surrounding Himalayan landscapes.
